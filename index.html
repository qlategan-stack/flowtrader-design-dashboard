System Overview
What FlowTrader is and how it works at a glance
🎯

Strategy

CLAUDE.md

Mean reversion — buys when an asset is statistically oversold relative to its short-term average, exits when price returns to the mean.

  • Indicators: Bollinger Bands, RSI, VWAP, ADX, ATR
  • Signal gate: 3 of 6 signals must fire
  • Target exit: 20-day moving average
🌍

Universe

config.yaml

Trades US equities and ETFs via Alpaca, and crypto via Bybit (CCXT). Equity and crypto watchlists are fully configurable.

  • Equities: NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMD, TSLA, META, SPY, QQQ, IWM, GLD, SLV, USO, TLT
  • Crypto: BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT, SOL/USDT, AVAX/USDT, LINK/USDT, DOGE/USDT
  • Brokers: Alpaca (equities), Bybit (crypto)
⚙️

Execution Mode

.env / secrets

Paper trading is the default and requires explicit dual-flag override to go live. Capital safety is the top priority.

  • Equities default: Paper trading on Alpaca sandbox
  • Crypto default: Bybit testnet
  • Live gate: Two env flags must both be set
  • Notifications: Telegram alerts on every trade

TradingDecisionAgent

agents/decision.py · claude-sonnet-4-6
Purpose

The brain of the system. Receives a full market snapshot (prices, indicators, news sentiment, account status) and uses the Claude claude-sonnet-4-6 model to output a structured JSON trade decision. The system prompt is loaded from CLAUDE.md, which encodes all hard rules and signal scoring definitions. Also loads the latest weekly research memo from journal/weekly_research_memo.json and injects a research context block into every prompt — including regime, mean reversion active flag, flagged opportunities, earnings avoidance, and risk warnings.

Key Methods
  • analyze_market(market_snapshot, account_snapshot) → dict
  • analyze_single_symbol(symbol_data, account) → dict
  • run_weekly_review(entries) → str
  • _build_research_context() → str # injects memo into prompt
  • _load_research_memo() → dict # loads weekly_research_memo.json
Output Format
BUY / SELL / HOLD / SKIP signal_score 0-6 entry + stop_loss + take_profit reasoning + journal_entry

OrderExecutor

agents/executor.py · Alpaca (equity) + Bybit via CCXT (crypto)
Purpose

Validates every trade decision against hard guardrails before submitting to Alpaca (equities) or Bybit (crypto). Routes orders based on whether the symbol contains a "/" (crypto) or not (equity). For crypto orders, fetches live Bybit balance to determine buying power and position count. Acts as a final safety layer — even if Claude returns a BUY, the executor will reject if any guardrail fails. Defaults to paper trading (Alpaca) and Bybit testnet (crypto).

Key Methods
  • validate_order(symbol, side, qty, entry, stop, acct_val, positions, day_pl) → (bool, str)
  • calculate_quantity(account_value, entry_price, stop_loss) → int
  • place_order(decision, account) → dict # routes equity or crypto
Guardrails Enforced
Max 3 positions 2% daily loss limit 1% max position risk Stop loss required Paper / testnet default

ResearchAnalyst

agents/researcher.py · claude-sonnet-4-6 + Alpha Vantage + yfinance
Purpose

Weekly market strategist that runs every Sunday evening. Scans a broad universe for mean reversion candidates, fetches VIX (via yfinance), all 11 sector ETF performances (via Alpha Vantage), the earnings calendar, and Alpaca market news. Sends everything to Claude to produce a structured JSON weekly memo with watchlist recommendations, sector focus, risk warnings, and a trading confidence score (1-10) for the coming week. The memo is stored at journal/weekly_research_memo.json and a Markdown copy at journal/weekly_research_memo.md. The Trading Decision Agent reads this memo at every session.

Key Methods
  • run_full_analysis(current_watchlist) → dict # summary
  • get_vix_level() → dict # uses yfinance ^VIX
  • get_sector_performance() → list # 11 sectors ranked
  • get_broader_universe_scan() → list # top 10 candidates
  • get_earnings_calendar() → list # 7-day lookahead
  • get_market_news_summary() → list # Alpaca news API
  • generate_weekly_memo(...) → dict # Claude's structured memo
  • save_memo(memo) → (json_path, md_path)
  • load_current_memo() → Optional[dict] # checks expiry

InStrategyAnalyst

agents/analyst_in.py · claude-sonnet-4-6
Purpose

Reviews the trade journal and produces parameter-tuning suggestions within the existing mean reversion strategy. Focuses on RSI thresholds, Bollinger Band settings, ADX filter, VWAP deviation, ATR multipliers, signal weighting, exit rules, and position sizing. Requires at least 10 journal entries. Suggestions are stored in journal/suggestions_in.jsonl and rendered in the dashboard.

Key Methods
  • run(days=30) → list[str] # suggestion IDs created/updated
  • _build_prompt(entries, claude_md, days) → str
  • _parse_suggestions(raw) → list[dict]
Suggestion Categories
rsi_threshold bollinger_params adx_filter vwap_deviation atr_multiplier signal_weight exit_rule position_sizing

OutStrategyAnalyst

agents/analyst_out.py · claude-sonnet-4-6 + yfinance macro
Purpose

Reviews the trade journal alongside live macro market context (VIX, SPY vs MA20, sector 5-day performance) and produces strategic suggestions that go beyond parameter tuning — regime fit, new complementary strategies, behavioral patterns, watchlist suitability, and risk framework improvements. Does NOT suggest mean reversion parameter changes (that is InStrategyAnalyst's domain). Suggestions stored in journal/suggestions_out.jsonl.

Key Methods

Signal Scoring System
A trade requires at least 3 points out of a possible 6 to enter
Minimum entry threshold: 3 points. This gates out weak setups and ensures multiple confirming indicators agree before capital is deployed. The threshold is tunable — the weekly research memo may recommend tightening it during high-volatility regimes. The InStrategyAnalyst reviews signal scoring effectiveness daily.
+2
RSI < 32Strong oversold — highest-weight signal
+1
RSI < 40Mild oversold — early stress indicator
+1
Below Lower Bollinger BandPrice outside 2σ — statistically oversold
+1
Below VWAP by > 1%Divergence from volume-weighted price
+1
Positive News SentimentAlpha Vantage sentiment score > 0.15 (equities only)
+1
ADX < 20 (Ranging)Market regime confirmed non-trending
Trend Filter (HARD BLOCK): If ADX > 25, mean reversion signals are entirely skipped — the trend filter overrides all signal scoring. Mean reversion in a trending market is the primary cause of strategy losses. This threshold is the primary target for InStrategyAnalyst tuning suggestions.
🛡️
Hard Rules — Never Violate
Encoded in CLAUDE.md and enforced by OrderExecutor. These cannot be overridden by Claude's reasoning.
1
Position Size ≤ 1%Never risk more than 1% of account value per trade. Position sizing is calculated at order time using risk-per-share = entry − stop.
2
Daily Loss Limit: −2%If day P&L hits −2% of portfolio, stop all trading. The bot shuts down until the next trading day.
3
Max 3 Open PositionsNever hold more than 3 simultaneous positions. Enforced by OrderExecutor before order submission (separately for equity and crypto).
4
Stop Loss Always SetEvery order must include a stop loss at order time. No order without a stop — ever. Equity orders use Alpaca bracket orders; crypto attaches a trigger order.
5
Time Gate: No new equity entries after 14:55 ESTNo overnight holds on equities. All equity positions closed before market close. Crypto runs 24/7.
6
Trend Filter: ADX > 25 → SkipIf ADX > 25 on any symbol, do NOT take mean reversion signals on that symbol.
7
Signal Threshold: 3 of 6Require at least 3 signal points before entering. Single-indicator setups are noise, not edge.
8
No Leverage — Spot OnlyNever trade on margin. Cash-funded spot positions only. Leverage multiplies losses in mean reversion drawdowns.
9
News Filter: Skip Earnings ±48hIf a symbol has earnings announced within 48 hours, skip it. Earnings crush mean reversion edge.
10
Paper First — AlwaysDefault is paper trading (Alpaca sandbox) and Bybit testnet for crypto. Two env flags must both be explicitly set to enable live equity trading.

Exit Rules
How and when positions are closed — partial, hard stop, time stop
🎯

Take Profit (Mean Reversion Target)

Exit target is the 20-day simple moving average — "the mean" the strategy is reverting to.

  • Partial exit at mean: Take 50% of the position off when price hits MA20
  • Trail remainder: Move stop to 0.25× ATR trailing stop on remaining 50%
  • Goal: Lock in the core gain while letting the winner run slightly
⛔

Stop Loss Rules

Two stop loss mechanisms protect capital:

  • Hard stop: Set at entry − 0.5 × ATR at order time — never moved down
  • Time stop: Exit any position open for more than 3 days regardless of P&L
  • ATR calibration: Stop width is proportional to the symbol's recent volatility
  • Equity: Alpaca bracket order handles stop automatically
  • Crypto: Separate Bybit trigger order placed after market order fills
